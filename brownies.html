Sweet Potato Brownie Recipe

This sweet potato brownie recipe comes from my older sister, who’s always looking for healthier versions of her favorite desserts. She made these one day and I was so skeptical at first—but once I tasted them, I was hooked. These brownies are rich, chocolatey, and honestly don’t even taste “healthy,” even though they totally are! The base is 1 cup of mashed sweet potato, which makes the brownies super moist. Mix that with 1/4 cup maple syrup (or agave) and 1/4 cup melted coconut oil (or other cooking oil). In the same bowl, add 1/2 cup cocoa powder, 1/4 cup whole wheat flour, and a pinch of salt. Stir everything together until it’s nice and smooth. Fold in 1/2 cup chocolate chips (or regular if that’s what you have). Pour the batter into a greased 8x8 pan and smooth it out evenly. Bake at 350°F for about 25 minutes, or until the top looks set. Let them cool before cutting—if you can wait, they taste even better after a few hours in the fridge. These are the kind of brownies you can eat without feeling guilty, but they still taste rich and chocolatey enough to satisfy any craving. I love having a batch of these on hand when I want something sweet but a little more balanced. Thanks to my sister for sharing one of her go-to healthy treats with me!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup mashed sweet potato
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup or agave
  • 1/4 cup melted coconut oil (or other cooking oil)
  • 1/2 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup whole wheat flour
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips (or regular chocolate chips)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F and grease an 8x8 inch baking pan.
  2. In a bowl, mix mashed sweet potato, maple syrup, and melted coconut oil.
  3. Add cocoa powder, whole wheat flour, and a pinch of salt. Stir until smooth.
  4. Fold in the chocolate chips.
  5. Pour batter into the prepared pan and spread evenly.
  6. Bake for about 25 minutes, or until the top looks set.
  7. Let cool before cutting. (They taste even better after a few hours in the fridge!)
